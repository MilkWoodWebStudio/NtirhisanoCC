Our Beliefs

This is a community-run centre dedicated to creating a safe, accessible, and empowering space for local communities. Based in Salt River, Cape Town, we believe in mutual aid, collective learning, and radical inclusion.
Our doors are open to all who seek connection, creativity, and liberation.

About NCC

Established in 2022, Ntirhisano Community Centre (NCC) has been participating in activities with grassroots communities and cultivating mutual aid principles that embrace self-reliance and offer alternatives to electoral politics. We are based in the Salt River community in Cape Town South Africa, and we also work with struggles in the broader Cape Town area, seeking to link the different struggles and to form connections and solidarities between movements which can otherwise be insular. We are also forming connections with similar spaces and organisations in South Africa as well as internationally too.

  • To Share

    A shared space that builds connection, belonging, and social cohesion.

  • Safe Space for the youth

    A rare, safe place for the youth to share, learn, and socialise.

What We Do

At NCC, we run a wide range of programs that respond directly to the needs of our community:

  • Free public Wi-Fi access

    Because digital access is a basic need.

  • Cowhide Open Mic & Poetry

    A recurring arts event platforming marginalised artists – a space where stories, struggles, and resistance take centre stage.

  • Radical Film Screening

    Curated political films followed by open discussions – creating space to imagine and debate for a different world.

  • Gate 7

    Support for young families and children, building community care from the ground up.

  • English language classes

    Especially important for migrant and refugee communities navigating daily life and bureaucracy.

  • Responsive programming

    We host community-led events, workshops, and gatherings as needs arise – from tenants’ rights meetings to healing circles.

We Need Your Support

We operate on a shoestring budget, with no government strings attached. This independence is crucial for protecting the integrity of our work and the safety of our communities.
